# Literature references

The PDFs of the foundational papers for this project were kept locally during development but are not redistributed in this repository. The citations below identify every source used in `docs/lit_review.md` and throughout the library documentation.

1. Rabiner, L. R. (1989). A tutorial on hidden Markov models and selected applications in speech recognition. *Proceedings of the IEEE*, 77(2), 257 to 286. DOI: 10.1109/5.18626

2. Durbin, R., Eddy, S. R., Krogh, A., and Mitchison, G. (1998). *Biological Sequence Analysis: Probabilistic Models of Proteins and Nucleic Acids*. Cambridge University Press. ISBN 978 0 521 62971 3

3. Eddy, S. R. (1998). Profile hidden Markov models. *Bioinformatics*, 14(9), 755 to 763. DOI: 10.1093/bioinformatics/14.9.755

4. Burge, C. and Karlin, S. (1997). Prediction of complete gene structures in human genomic DNA. *Journal of Molecular Biology*, 268(1), 78 to 94. DOI: 10.1006/jmbi.1997.0951

5. Salzberg, S. L., Delcher, A. L., Kasif, S., and White, O. (1998). Microbial gene identification using interpolated Markov models (Glimmer). *Nucleic Acids Research*, 26(2), 544 to 548. DOI: 10.1093/nar/26.2.544

6. Kulp, D., Haussler, D., Reese, M. G., and Eeckman, F. H. (1996). A generalized hidden Markov model for the recognition of human genes in DNA. *Proceedings of the International Conference on Intelligent Systems for Molecular Biology*, 4, 134 to 142.

7. Stanke, M. and Waack, S. (2003). Gene prediction with a hidden Markov model and a new intron submodel. *Bioinformatics*, 19 (Supplement 2), ii215 to ii225. DOI: 10.1093/bioinformatics/btg1080

8. Stanke, M., Keller, O., Gunduz, I., Hayes, A., Waack, S., and Morgenstern, B. (2006). AUGUSTUS: ab initio prediction of alternative transcripts. *Nucleic Acids Research*, 34(Web Server), W435 to W439. DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkl200

9. Tu, S. Derivation of the Baum Welch algorithm for hidden Markov models. Lecture notes.
